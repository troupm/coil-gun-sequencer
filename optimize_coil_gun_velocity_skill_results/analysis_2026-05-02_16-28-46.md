## Data-Quality Notes (read first)

Two structural issues are distorting the auto-analysis:

1. **Yardstick contamination of the primary metric.** Sequence `73b2e2d9` (2026-04-29, 26 runs) used a 574 mm "yardstick" projectile for gate-distance calibration. Its gate transit times are 41–98 m/s simply because the projectile is ~60× longer than the real one (9.5 mm). The script auto-selected `gate_2_transit_velocity_ms` as the primary metric because it has the most coverage, and the top-quartile partition collapses to "the yardstick runs" (top median 79.6 vs rest 1.48 m/s). Any t-stat near ±20 in the primary-metric profile is the yardstick split, not a real config signal. **Ignore those.**
2. **Gate-transit metrics under active investigation.** A negative-transit-time issue is being reproduced in the field as of this session (post-conversation context). Until the cause is found and historic rows reprocessed, every `gate_N_transit_velocity_ms` value should be treated as suspect. Flight metrics (leading-edge → leading-edge between gates) are unaffected because they don't use the trailing edge.
3. **Stale coil R/L logged.** DB shows `coil_1_resistance` ∈ {1.3, 14.4, 14.5} Ω and `coil_3_resistance` ∈ {5.0, 5.8} Ω. Operator-provided current specs are **coil 1: 4.1 Ω / 5.2 mH; coil 2: 5.7 Ω / 2.5 mH; coil 3: 16 Ω / 6 mH**. The logged values are metadata only (not in the firing path) but they make the auto-analysis confound coil-spec changes with regime changes. The Coil-Electromagnetics table below uses operator-provided specs.

Net: trust top-quartile profiles for **flight metrics**, ignore the primary-metric profile, and treat every recommendation below as conditional on the negative-transit investigation.

## Coil Electromagnetics (using operator-provided current specs)

| Stage | R (Ω) | L (µH) | τ = L/R (µs) | Pulse range tested (µs) | Pulse/τ range | I_peak @ 62 V (A) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Coil 1 | 4.1 | 5200 | **1268** | 5000–8100 | 3.9–6.4 τ | 15.1 |
| Coil 2 | 5.7 | 2500 | **439** | 5000, 6960 | 11.4 τ, 15.9 τ | 10.9 |
| Coil 3 | 16.0 | 6000 | **375** | 5000, 5300, 7000 | 13.3, 14.1, 18.7 τ | 3.9 |

**Every stage is being driven well past 3τ.** The 1–3τ "sweet spot" windows are:

- Coil 1: **1270–3800 µs** (currently 5000+)
- Coil 2: **440–1320 µs** (currently 5000+, ~4× the upper bound)
- Coil 3: **375–1130 µs** (currently 5000+, ~4× the upper bound)

In push mode with neodymium magnets the over-driven pulse doesn't reverse the projectile (force is monotonically forward on the +z side), so the penalty is energy waste and capacitor-bank sag, not suck-back. Per-coil 4000 µF banks at 62 V hold 7.7 J; a 5000 µs steady-state pulse on coil 2 dissipates ~3.4 J — about 44 % of the bank in heat after the projectile has already left the field.

The top-quartile signal "shorter coil-2 pulse wins" (5000 < 6960) is consistent with the physics. The data hasn't tested *much* shorter pulses yet — that's the biggest open lever.

## Next Field Test Plan (5 suggested runs, in order)

The two highest-leverage knobs are (1) the gate→coil delays, where the data has a clear "shorter is better" gradient and the floor of the explored range is still suspicious, and (2) coil-2 / coil-3 pulse durations, which haven't been tested anywhere near the 1–3τ sweet spot the physics predicts.

| # | Goal | `gate_1_coil_2_delay_us` | `gate_2_coil_3_delay_us` | `coil_1_pulse_us` | `coil_2_pulse_us` | `coil_3_pulse_us` | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Anchor — current data-driven optimum | 6000 | 4500 | 6000 | 5000 | 5300 | v_ceil 70, offset 0 |
| 2 | Push delays below tested floor | **5000** | **3500** | 6000 | 5000 | 5300 | (else identical to #1) |
| 3 | Coil 2 in-physics pulse window | 6000 | 4500 | 6000 | **1500** (≈3.4τ) | 5300 | (else identical to #1) |
| 4 | Coil 3 in-physics pulse window | 6000 | 4500 | 6000 | 5000 | **1000** (≈2.7τ) | (else identical to #1) |
| 5 | Combined short-pulse, short-delay | 5000 | 3500 | **3500** (≈2.8τ) | **1500** | **1000** | High-leverage but high-variance |

Rationale, per row:

1. **Anchor** — fixes the operating point at the data's current "best-known" intersection so subsequent runs are evaluated against a known reference. Expected G1→G2 ≥ 5.4, G2→G3 ≥ 6.5 if the signal is real.
2. **Below-floor delays** — top-quartile means are 6106 and 4550 µs, both at the low end of the tested range. If "shorter wins" is monotonic, going lower should keep paying. If it stops paying or reverses at 5000/3500, that's the optimum cliff and we've found the bottom.
3. **Coil-2 short pulse** — current 5000 µs is 11.4τ; 1500 µs is 3.4τ. Saves ≈3 J of cap-bank energy per shot. Push-mode physics says the projectile already has the field it needs after a few τ; the rest is heating the winding.
4. **Coil-3 short pulse** — current 5300 µs is 14.1τ; 1000 µs is 2.7τ. Same rationale; coil 3's I_peak is only 3.9 A so the dissipation argument is weaker, but pulse-truncation noise should still be testable.
5. **All-together** — if rows 2–4 each show ≥0.2 m/s improvement, this should compound. Also a good place to land if the operator has limited test budget.

**Also strongly recommended for the same session:**
- **Re-log coil R/L** with current values (4.1/5200, 5.7/2500, 16/6000) so future analyses correlate against reality.
- **Resolve the negative-transit issue** before drawing further conclusions from gate-transit metrics. Capture raw `t_gate_N_on/off` ns timestamps for any negative-transit run; the magnitude tells us whether it's an EMI glitch (sub-µs) or a physical edge-mapping issue (hundreds of µs).

Under-explored knobs not in the plan above (deferred for variance budget):

- `coil_N_brake_resistor_ohms` (only one value each).

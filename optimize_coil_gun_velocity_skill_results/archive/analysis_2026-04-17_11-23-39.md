### Push-Mode Electromagnetics Notes

**The 1-3tau "sweet spot" does not apply in push mode.** The classical reasoning was: pulse < tau = under-magnetised, pulse > 3tau = wasted energy + suck-back. With suck-back eliminated:

- **Minimum useful pulse** is still ~1-2tau (the field needs time to ramp up to useful levels)
- **Maximum useful pulse** is now bounded by **projectile dwell time in the exit half of the bore**, not by suck-back onset
- Excess pulse beyond dwell time wastes energy as resistive heating but causes no retarding force

**Coil 2 time-constant is problematic for push mode.** At tau=679us, the field takes ~2000us to reach 95% saturation. By the time the field is strong, the projectile (traveling ~3-5 m/s) has already covered 6-10mm. If the exit half of the bore is ~10-15mm, a significant fraction of the push window is spent at sub-optimal field strength. A coil with **shorter tau** (lower L/R) would deliver force faster.

**Brake resistor implications reversed.** In pull mode, fast freewheel decay (high R_brake) was desirable to truncate suck-back. In push mode, the residual field after turn-off **continues to push** (projectile is past midpoint), so **slower decay (lower R_brake) is now beneficial.** Currently: coil 1 = 1 ohm, coil 2 = 2 ohm. Testing 0 ohm on coil 2 would extend the push tail.

### Observations

1. **Delay sweet spot is 4020-5000 us.** 6000 is clearly too late. Within 4020-5000, G1->G2 velocity is similar (~5.0-5.2), but G2 transit velocity improves monotonically as delay decreases (1.04 -> 1.18 -> 1.32). This suggests the projectile is still gaining speed as coil 2 fires earlier — the midpoint timing target may be closer to 4000 us than 5000 us.

2. **c2p=4960 vs 6960 is within noise.** The 6960 runs (pulse/tau=10.3) show no improvement over 4960 (pulse/tau=7.3). Both are well past saturation. This confirms that beyond ~3tau, additional pulse duration has negligible effect in push mode — the field is already at steady state and the projectile exits the bore before the extra time matters.

3. **g2c3 delay trends lower = better for G2 transit** (4000 > 5000 > 7000), but coil 3 isn't attached, so this only measures g2c3's effect on measurement timing, not actual acceleration. Not actionable until coil 3 is installed.

## Oscilloscope Trace Comparison

Two scope captures were provided (Hantek DSO2C10, 2-channel).

**Probe assignment (operator-confirmed):**
- **CH1 (yellow):** Collector-Emitter voltage (V_CE) probe on the **Coil 1 IGBT** (low-side switch)
- **CH2 (green):** Collector-Emitter voltage (V_CE) probe on the **Coil 2 IGBT** (low-side switch)

These are NOT capacitor bank voltage probes. They show IGBT switching waveforms: V_CE drops to saturation (~1-3 V) when the IGBT turns on and current flows through the coil, then spikes above V_rail at turn-off as the collapsing magnetic field drives the flyback/brake circuit (V_CE = V_rail + I_coil x R_brake).

### Trace 1 — Shared Capacitor Bank, Old Timing (`data/sequence_traces/shared_capacitor_bank.jpg`)

- **Time base:** 10 ms/div
- **CH1 (yellow, 2 V/div):** Coil 1 IGBT V_CE
- **CH2 (green, 200 mV/div):** Coil 2 IGBT V_CE (at a much smaller vertical scale)

**Observations:**

1. **Coil 1 IGBT switching clearly visible on CH1.** Sharp drop (IGBT turns ON, V_CE falls to saturation), flat low period (coil energised for the pulse duration), then a sharp upward spike at turn-off — this is the **flyback overshoot**, where V_CE = V_rail + I_coil x R_brake. The spike height (roughly 0.5-1 div above baseline at this scale/attenuation) gives a rough measure of the actual peak current at turn-off.
2. **Coil 2 pulse barely visible on CH2.** With the old config's near-zero delay (1-110 us), both coils fire almost simultaneously. At 10 ms/div, they appear coincident. The small CH2 disturbance aligns with the CH1 event. The much smaller CH2 scale (200 mV/div vs 2 V/div) makes direct comparison difficult — the probes may use different attenuation settings.
3. **Single composite switching event.** Because both coils fire at virtually the same instant in the old config, the two V_CE waveforms overlap temporally. This makes it impossible to distinguish individual coil behavior from this capture.

### Trace 2 — Per-Coil Capacitor Banks, Push-Push Timing (`data/sequence_traces/per_coil_capacitor_banks.jpg`)

- **Time base:** 5 ms/div
- **CH1 (yellow):** Coil 1 IGBT V_CE
- **CH2 (green):** Coil 2 IGBT V_CE

**Observations:**

1. **Two clearly separated IGBT switching events.** CH1 shows coil 1 firing first; CH2 shows coil 2 firing ~4-5 ms later. At 5 ms/div, the delay spans approximately one full division — **directly confirming the push-push gate_1_coil_2_delay_us (4000-5000 us) in hardware.**
2. **Well-defined flyback spikes on both channels.** Each IGBT shows a distinct turn-off overshoot. The spike magnitude is proportional to I_coil x R_brake at turn-off, providing a visual indicator of whether the coil reached its expected peak current.
3. **Independent switching behavior.** With per-coil banks and the push-push delay, the two IGBTs operate completely independently — coil 1 has finished its entire pulse and flyback decay before coil 2 even begins. This temporal isolation is a direct consequence of the push-push timing and eliminates any electrical cross-talk between stages.
4. **Coil 2 pulse shape appears clean.** The CH2 waveform shows a well-defined V_CE(sat) period followed by a flyback spike, indicating that coil 2's IGBT is switching cleanly and the coil is reaching meaningful current before turn-off.

### Corrected Implications for Velocity Optimization

These V_CE traces show **IGBT switching waveforms**, not capacitor bank voltage. They do NOT directly reveal bank voltage sag or resistive wiring losses. What they DO reveal:

| Observable | What it tells us | Implication |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Flyback spike height (V_CE overshoot at turn-off) | Proportional to I_coil x R_brake — indicates actual peak current at turn-off | Can validate whether coil is reaching theoretical I_peak |
| V_CE(sat) duration | The IGBT on-time = coil pulse duration | Confirms firmware timing matches config |
| Temporal separation (trace 2) | The push-push delay is working as configured | Visual confirmation of 4-5 ms inter-coil delay |
| Coil 2 switching quality (trace 2 vs trace 1) | Coil 2 shows a clean, independent switching event in the per-coil config | Suggests coil 2 is operating normally with its dedicated bank |

**What we CANNOT conclude from these traces** (correcting earlier analysis):
- ~~Capacitor bank voltage sag magnitude~~ — would need probes on the bank terminals
- ~~Resistive IR drop in shared wiring~~ — would need probes at different circuit nodes
- ~~Per-coil bank producing "shallower sag"~~ — V_CE(sat) is an IGBT property, not a bank property

**What remains true from other evidence:**
- The push-push regime with per-coil banks halved CV (10.4% vs 18.2%) — this is run-data, not scope-dependent
- Per-coil banks eliminate shared-bank depletion coupling between stages — this is circuit-design fact
- The energy calculation (<7% bank depletion per pulse) still holds — the bank sag question just isn't answered by these V_CE probes

**Recommended next scope measurement:** To directly observe capacitor bank behavior, probe V_rail at each bank's output terminals (not V_CE). This would show actual voltage sag during the pulse and recovery time, answering whether the per-coil bank advantage comes from reduced sag, reduced wiring IR drop, or both.

### Energy Analysis (from first principles, not scope-derived)

With V_rail = 62 V and per-coil banks at 4000 uF, stored energy per bank is 7.69 J. The coil energy consumed per pulse is approximately `E_coil = 0.5 * L * I^2` at turn-off:

- **Coil 1:** At 13.9 tau, current is at full saturation: `I = 47.7 A`, so `E_coil = 0.5 * 476e-6 * 47.7^2 = 0.54 J`. This is only **7% of the bank's stored energy**.
- **Coil 2:** At 7.3 tau, current is at full saturation: `I = 22.1 A`, so `E_coil = 0.5 * 1900e-6 * 22.1^2 = 0.46 J`. Only **6% of the bank's energy**.

Both coils draw <10% of their bank's stored energy. Capacitor depletion alone should produce minimal voltage sag (~3-5% V_rail drop). However, **resistive losses in bus wiring and IGBT V_CE(sat)** also draw from the bank during the pulse — coil 1 at 47.7 A through even 0.1 ohm of total path resistance dissipates ~228 W continuously, causing additional sag that compounds over the pulse duration. In the shared-bank configuration, both coils' wiring paths converge on a single bank, concentrating IR drops. Per-coil banks eliminate this shared-path coupling.

**To quantify actual bank sag, a V_rail probe (on the bank terminals, not V_CE) would be needed** — see scope recommendation above. The V_CE traces confirm IGBT switching is clean but cannot distinguish between the bank-sag and wiring-resistance hypotheses.

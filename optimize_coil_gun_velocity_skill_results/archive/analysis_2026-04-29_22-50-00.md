## ⚠️ Data Anomaly — `projectile_length_mm = 574` in `73b2e2d9`

The new sequence logs a projectile length of **574 mm** on every run. Prior
sequences logged 5.4 mm or 7.4 mm. Three pieces of evidence say this is a
data-entry error, not a real 574 mm rod:

1. **Internal inconsistency.** `gate_1_transit_velocity_ms` (computed as
   `projectile_length / (gate_off - gate_on)`) reports 41–96 m/s, while
   `gate_1_to_gate_2_velocity_ms` (computed from the 100 mm gate spacing,
   not from the entered length) reports a stable 4.30 ± 0.42 m/s. If the
   projectile actually moved at ~70 m/s, the gate-to-gate metric would
   show ~70 m/s, not 4.3 m/s. They disagree by a factor of ~16×.

2. **Mass.** `projectile_mass_grams = 1.15 g`. A 574 mm steel rod the
   diameter of any prior projectile would weigh dozens of grams. 1.15 g
   is consistent with a small neodymium magnet (similar to the 2.08 g
   magnet from the prior session).

3. **Top-quartile profile is dominated by the length jump.** Every metric
   shows `projectile_length_mm` as the strongest predictor (t-stat 25–28),
   with top-mean 574 vs rest-mean ~58. This is not a physics signal — it
   is the cross-session length step swamping every within-session signal.

**Action for the operator:** Verify the projectile actually used in
sequence `73b2e2d9` and correct the snapshot column. Until corrected, the
"primary" `gate_1_transit_velocity_ms` numbers and any top-quartile
profile that ranks projectile geometry first are unreliable. The
geometry-derived `gate_1_to_gate_2_velocity_ms` and
`gate_2_to_gate_3_velocity_ms` are unaffected by the length error and are
what I use below.

## Coil Electromagnetics Table

DB-recorded ratings (constant across all 91 runs in the dataset; the
operator's "real" coil values from the prior session memory note —
4.1/5.7/16 Ω, 5.2/2.5/6 mH — have not been migrated into snapshots).

| Stage | R (Ω) | L (µH) | τ = L/R (µs) | Pulse (µs) | Pulse / τ | I_peak = 62 V / R (A) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Coil 1 | 1.3 | 476  | **366**  | 8100 | **22.1×** | 47.7 |
| Coil 2 | 2.8 | 1900 | **679**  | 6960 | **10.3×** | 22.1 |
| Coil 3 | 5.0 | 1000 | **200**  | 5300 | **26.5×** | 12.4 |

**Caveat 1 — magnet projectile:** With a neodymium-magnet projectile,
τ_eff ≈ τ_air (no µ_r boost from a ferromagnetic slug), so these ratios
are honest. With a soft-iron slug all three would be larger by µ_r ×.

**Caveat 2 — push-mode dwell, not L/R, is the binding constraint.** In
the current push-push-push regime the projectile is past the coil
midpoint at fire and is being shoved further away. Pulse duration should
match **dwell time** (how long the magnet remains close enough for the
field to do useful work, ~1–10 mm of axial travel) and not the L/R
sweet-spot. At a stage-1 projectile speed of ~4 m/s, traversing 10 mm of
useful coupling distance takes 2.5 ms, so coil-1 at 8.1 ms is **3×
longer than the useful coupling window** and the extra on-time is
heating winding + bleeding cap energy. Same logic puts coil-2 at ~2× and
coil-3 at ~1.3× the useful pulse window (using observed gate-to-gate
speeds).

**Caveat 3 — current starvation on coil 3.** I_peak = 12.4 A on coil 3
vs 47.7 A on coil 1 means coil 3 produces ~25% of coil 1's field for
the same on-time (B ∝ N·I, all else equal). Combined with the shortest
τ (200 µs), coil 3 is doing the least work in the chain. The +1.5 m/s
G2→G3 vs G1→G2 average velocity is consistent with coil 3 contributing
*something*, but it's the weakest of the three.

## Recommendations

### 1. Optimal Config (Best Known) — High Confidence

Use the table above as-is. It is the top-5-median across the most recent
session and matches the only convincing A/B signal in the dataset
(g2c3 ≥ ~7000 µs).

### 2. Coil Electromagnetics Summary

All three coils run pulses 10–26× their L/R time-constant. With a
magnet projectile in push mode this is **not destructive** (no suck-back),
but it is wasteful: stage-1 dwell time ~2.5 ms vs 8.1 ms pulse means ~70%
of coil-1 on-time is post-coupling resistive heating. The data does not
yet contain any pulse-duration sweep on coils 2 or 3, so the cost of
this waste in terms of cap-bank sag is unquantified.
